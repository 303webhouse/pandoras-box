ETF MARKET STRUCTURE EXPLAINED

Understanding Primary vs Secondary Markets, Creation/Redemption Mechanics, and Why Volume ≠ Flows
THE TWO MARKETS

1. SECONDARY MARKET

What you see on screen

  • Where you and I trade ETF shares on an exchange
  • You click buy on the BTC ETF, someone sells to you
  • Just trading existing shares
  • Does NOT automatically mean new BTC was bought by the ETF
Bottom Line: Traders trading with each other

2. PRIMARY MARKET

Where creations and redemptions happen

  • Between the ETF and authorized participants/market makers
  • Where new ETF shares get created
  • Where old shares get taken out
  • This is what people mean by "flows"
Bottom Line: ETF plumbing
WHEN DOES A CREATION HAPPEN?
KEY PRINCIPLE
An ETF share should trade very close to its net asset value. For a BTC ETF, that is basically the value of the bitcoin it holds.

If there is a lot of buying in the secondary market, the ETF price might start trading a little rich. A market maker can then do this:

Creation Process

1
Sell ETF shares to buyers in the market, even if it does not have them yet. Now it is short ETF shares.
2
Go to the ETF in the primary market and say, create for me one creation unit.
3
To get that creation unit, it delivers the basket, for spot BTC ETFs that usually means delivering bitcoin.
4
The ETF hands back newly created ETF shares.
5
The market maker uses those shares to flatten its short.

Result: That new block of shares is a creation, that shows up as positive flow. The ETF now holds more BTC than before.

So, net buying pressure in the secondary market can lead to primary creations, but only when the market maker needs to refill.

WHEN DOES A REDEMPTION HAPPEN?

Flip it.

Redemption Process

1
There is more selling than buying in the ETF.
2
A market maker keeps buying ETF shares from sellers, so now it is long extra ETF shares.
3
Instead of dumping them in the market and pushing price lower, it can go to the ETF and redeem them.
4
It gives the ETF a big block of ETF shares, the ETF gives back the underlying basket, for BTC that means bitcoin, or sometimes cash.
5
That is a redemption, that shows up as negative flow.

Result: So, redemptions shrink the ETF, creations grow it.

WHY VOLUME AND FLOWS DO NOT MATCH

Common Misconception

Your community sees 2 billion traded and thinks 2 billion of bitcoin was bought. No.

If Alice buys 100 shares and Bob sells 100 shares, that is 100 shares of volume, but no creation. The ETF did not have to do anything.

CRITICAL INSIGHT
The ETF only moves when the market maker's inventory gets offside enough that it has to go to the primary market.

Timing Quirk

Most spot bitcoin ETFs settle primary creations in T+1, normal exchange trades settle in T+2. So a market maker that sold shares today might wait until tomorrow to create. That is why you can see heavy buying on day 1, and the creation shows up on day 2.

WHAT "FLOWS" ACTUALLY TELL YOU
Flows Tell You

How much new capital had to enter the ETF structure.

Creations Mean

New money came in, the ETF had to acquire more BTC.

Redemptions Mean

Money left, the ETF gave back BTC or cash.

Volume Only Tells You

Trading interest.
