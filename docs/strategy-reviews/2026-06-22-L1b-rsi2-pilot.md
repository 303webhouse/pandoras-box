## 1. Factor

Connors RSI-2 mean-reversion. Buy short-term oversold dips inside a confirmed uptrend on liquid index ETFs; exit on the snap-back. Long-only by construction (the 200-SMA trend filter removes the short side; the symmetric short is a separate spec).

## 2. Spec v0.1

- **Universe:** SPY, QQQ (pilot). Liquid index ETFs by design — no single-name idiosyncratic risk.
- **Trend filter:** Close > 200-day SMA (long only in an uptrend).
- **Entry:** RSI(2) < 10  (tighter variant: < 5).
- **Exit (primary):** Close > 5-day SMA.
- **Exit (alt):** RSI(2) > 70.
- **Catastrophic stop:** Close < 200-day SMA -> flat (regime invalidation).
- **Failsafe:** max hold 10 trading days.
- **Eval:** daily on close; close-to-close fills, slippage not modeled (Stage-2 concern).
- **Timeframe / hold:** swing, ~1-5 days (avg 3-4 observed) -> B2 bucket.
- **signal_category:** Mean Reversion lens, +GEX-gated. Exact enum confirmed from scorer at build-brief stage.

Two authoring decisions flagged for committee (URSA): (a) entry 5 vs 10; (b) stops — Connors tested stopless; the 200-SMA-break is the least-bad live backstop, but the stopless version may backtest cleaner and is not live-deployable as-is.

## 4. Stage-1 backtest — canon

**Method:** pure Python, yfinance daily bars (stooq is blocked behind a JS wall — do not use it), SPY + QQQ, 2000-01-03 -> 2026-06-22, 6,656 bars each. auto_adjust=True (split/div-adjusted). Long-only, one position per ticker, close-to-close fills, no slippage. Data validated vs outside sources (SPY ~744, QQQ ~738 on 2026-06-22). Script: `C:\temp\rsi2_backtest.py`.

**Results (pooled SPY+QQQ, underlying %):**

| Entry | Exit | Trades | Win% | PF | Avg/trade | MaxDD |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| RSI(2) <5 | close>5SMA | 203 | 78.8% | 2.36 | +0.59% | -11.4% |
| RSI(2) <5 | RSI(2)>70 | 202 | 79.7% | 2.42 | +0.74% | -11.0% |
| RSI(2) <10 | close>5SMA | 390 | 72.1% | 2.05 | +0.48% | -14.6% |
| RSI(2) <10 | RSI(2)>70 | 377 | 74.8% | 2.14 | +0.58% | -15.0% |

SPY and QQQ individually both land in the 71-82% win / 2.0-2.5 PF band (not a one-ticker fluke). In-market ~6-12% of days. Avg MFE ~+1.5-2.2% / avg MAE ~-1.2-1.9%.

**Read:** Edge durable across 26 years (dot-com unwind, GFC, COVID, 2022 bear) — the 200-SMA filter held. Positive expectancy in every config. Textbook mean-reversion shape: frequent small wins (~1.2-1.7%), rarer larger losses (~-1.5 to -2.6%); the 70%+ hit rate is what pays. Tighter <5 = cleaner/higher-conviction; <10 = ~2x trades, still 71-77% win. RSI>70 exit edges the 5-SMA exit by letting winners run ~1 extra day. Final param pick = Stage-2 tuning.

**Caveat (the important part):** these are RAW returns, NOT beta-stripped — and beta-stripped alpha is the metric that matters per the June-5 audit. We are in a parabolic up-tape; buying dips above the 200-SMA in a relentless bull wins partly because the market goes up. Encouraging that it is not ALL beta: ~6-12% market exposure, drawdown a fraction of buy-and-hold (-15% worst vs B&H eating 30-55% in '08/'20/'22), positive expectancy in every regime — roughly 2x the return per unit of market exposure. But genuine alpha in options-P&L is unproven here.
