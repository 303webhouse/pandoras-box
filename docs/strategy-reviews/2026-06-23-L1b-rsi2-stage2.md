## 1. What this measures

Stage-1 graded RSI-2 on the **underlying** (71–82% win, PF 2.0–2.5, survives 2000–2026 — commit `2d7d0eb`). Stage-2 asks the platform's real promotion question: **does that edge survive when expressed as the options structure we'd actually trade** — a 30-delta call debit spread, BS-priced with separate entry/exit IV — and is there **alpha left after stripping market beta**?

Model: per trade, a long 30-delta call + short (long + width) call (b2 width tiers $100→$5 / $20→$2.5 / else $1). Entry priced with entry-date IV, exit RE-priced with **exit-date IV** (the load-bearing rule — RSI-2 enters on elevated-IV dips and exits on the IV-normalizing snap-back). 1 contract, BS-mid, no slippage (blessed for SPY/QQQ). Engine = the live `bs_computed` engine (`backend/utils/options_math.py`); expressions = the live b2 collector's rules (`backend/jobs/b2_options_resolver.py`) — historical and forward stay apples-to-apples.

**IV tiering** (Step-0 probe): UW historical IV is plan-gated to the most recent 180 trading days → earliest = **2025-10-02 = the wall**. `entry_date ≥ wall` → Tier B (UW `interpolated-iv` at the trade DTE); else → Tier A (VIX/SPY, VXN/QQQ close ÷ 100, yfinance). The 2003–2026 ledger is ~95% Tier A; the ~8.5-month Tier-B window (12–17 entries/config) is the validation overlap.

## 2. Options-P&L — full history, ungated (the headline)

Pooled SPY+QQQ, 1 contract/trade, $ per spread:

| Config | Trades | Win% | PF | Avg/trade | **Total options-P&L** | MaxDD |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| RSI(2)<5, exit close>5SMA **(canonical)** | 203 | 54.2% | 0.99 | **−$0.14** | **−$28** | −$635 |
| RSI(2)<5, exit RSI>70 | 202 | 51.0% | 1.01 | +$0.31 | +$62 | −$970 |
| RSI(2)<10, exit close>5SMA | 390 | 48.7% | 0.83 | −$3.63 | −$1,416 | — |
| RSI(2)<10, exit RSI>70 | 377 | 48.0% | 0.82 | −$4.60 | −$1,733 | — |

**Read:** the Stage-1 edge collapses on translation. Win% falls **71–82% → 48–54%**; PF **2.0–2.5 → 0.82–1.01**. Absolute options-P&L is **negative in 3 of 4 configs**; the only positive (entry5_rsi70 +$62 over 202 trades = +$0.31/trade) is statistical noise. The canonical Connors config is **breakeven-negative (−$28)**.

**Mechanism (DAEDALUS, confirmed):** a 30-delta debit spread is net-long-vega. RSI-2 buys when IV is elevated (the fear dip) and exits when IV normalizes (the snap-back) — so **vega works against the trade**, and theta + the spread's cost-of-entry consume the small (~0.5–0.7%/trade) mean-reversion move. The underlying's directional edge is real but too small to clear the options friction.

## 3. Beta-stripped alpha (same-model SPY control)

For each factor trade, the identical structure is run on **SPY over the same [entry,exit] window**; alpha = factor − control. (Per the brief: a regression beta on option P&L is invalid; this same-model control is the honest strip. For SPY factor trades the window is SPY's own, so those net ≈ 0 by construction; aggregate alpha is driven by QQQ's excess + structural differences.)

| Config | Factor P&L | Control (SPY) P&L | **Alpha** | Alpha PF |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| canonical (entry5_sma5) | −$28 | +$115 | **−$142** | 0.90 |
| entry5_rsi70 | +$62 | −$2 | +$64 | 1.04 |
| entry10_sma5 | −$1,416 | −$1,942 | +$526 | 1.26 |
| entry10_rsi70 | −$1,733 | −$2,471 | +$738 | 1.33 |

**Read — the trap, called out:** the entry10 configs show *positive* alpha (+$526/+$738) **only because the SPY control over those same windows lost even more**. "Lost less than a same-structure SPY spread" is not promotable — the factor still bleeds money absolutely. The **canonical config's alpha is negative (−$142, PF 0.90)**. There is no config that is both absolutely profitable and positive-alpha. **No durable options alpha.**

**DAEDALUS sanity check (passes):** clean alpha is *far below*, not above, the Stage-1 underlying edge — the expected direction once options cost + the vega headwind are applied. The model is behaving sanely, not inventing edge.

## 5. Validation error band (Step 5 — non-arbitrable) — and the P0 it caught

**First run FAILED the gate** and that is the headline process win. 5b (modeled-vs-real) showed **40% mean error** — BS-mid systematically under-pricing real spreads ~$1.2 vs ~$2.0. Root cause, proven on the 2025-10-10 SPY trade: the harness priced options on yfinance **dividend-adjusted** closes (spot 647.65) while the real contracts traded against the **actual** SPY (~654; the real 662C's IV was *lower* than modeled yet its price *higher* — only possible at a higher true spot). Options trade vs the **unadjusted** underlying; the adjustment understates spot ~1% in 2025 and **~30% in 2003** (20+ yrs of SPY dividends), and the leveraged spread amplifies it. **Fix:** price on raw (unadjusted) closes for factor, control, and expiry settlement.

**Post-fix band (within gate):**

| Check | Result | Gate (~20%) |
|---|---|---|
| **5a proxy(VIX/VXN)-vs-UW** (bounds Tier-A error) | n=34, mean abs ΔP&L $13.94 = **11.1% of premium** | PASS |
| **5b modeled(BS-mid)-vs-real UW marks** | n=7, **15.6% mean** / 24.9% max abs err on entry spread | PASS (mean) |

The residual ~15% (5b) is **skew** — flat ATM IV vs real downside-skew (real per-strike IV 0.171/0.160 vs ATM 0.189). The brief's ">5% overlap → add 25-delta `historical_risk_reversal_skew`" refinement is now indicated for a **v2** (would tighten the band; would *not* rescue the verdict — see below).

**Does the band threaten the verdict? No.** The error band is 11–16%; the edge *decay* is an order of magnitude larger (underlying PF 2.0–2.5 → options PF 0.82–1.01). No 15% pricing-error correction turns a PF-0.9 strategy into a promotable one. The qualitative call is robust; only the precise $ magnitude carries the ±11–16% band.

## 6. Verdict — **RSI-2 FAILS Stage-2 vs a neutral baseline**

The underlying edge does **not** survive expression as a 30-delta debit spread: breakeven-negative absolute options-P&L (canonical −$28), no positive-alpha-and-profitable config, win/PF collapse driven by the mean-reversion vega headwind. **Do not promote RSI-2 as a debit-spread L1b factor.**

**Caveats bounding the verdict (honest):**
- Tested ONE expression (30-delta debit spread). RSI-2's edge is short-hold mean reversion; it may express better as a structure that is **not net-long-vega** (e.g. shorting a put spread / put credit on the dip, or long shares/long single call held briefly) — a different Stage-2 expression, a separate question. This run rejects the *debit-spread* expression, which is the b2 live structure.
