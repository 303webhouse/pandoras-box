## 1. Factor

12-1 momentum (Jegadeesh-Titman / Asness; Moskowitz-Ooi-Pedersen for the time-series form). Two mechanically distinct arms under one canon: **TS** (time-series / absolute, own-sign trend) and **XS** (cross-sectional / relative-strength ranking across a basket). Monthly rebalance, ~1-month hold, formation = trailing 12-month return skipping the most recent month (the skip dodges short-term reversal).

## 2. Spec v0.1

- **Universe (TS):** SPY, QQQ — liquid index ETFs, same liquid-by-design scope as the RSI-2 pilot.
- **Universe (XS):** SPDR sector basket — XLK XLF XLE XLV XLY XLP XLI XLU XLB XLRE XLC. Cross-section required for ranking. Dynamic universe (9 core sectors from 1998, XLRE from 2015, XLC from 2018; rank only names with full formation history at each rebalance).
- **Signal:** 12-1 = total return from t-252d to t-21d on adjusted close (auto_adjust=True -> dividend-inclusive), ~21 td/month.
- **Rebalance / hold:** monthly at month-end close; hold to next month-end.
- **Selection (TS, long-only):** long next month if own 12-1 > 0; else flat/cash (0%). Variant: > T-bill hurdle. Short side a separate spec.
- **Selection (XS):** rank basket by 12-1. Long-only = top quartile equal-weight; long/short = long top Q, short bottom Q, dollar-neutral. Cutoff = quartile (decile too thin on an 11-ETF basket).
- **Exit/stop:** time-based (re-rank monthly; names leaving the long set exit). Canon momentum is stopless intra-month; a live version needs a backstop — cleanest via defined-risk options structure (flagged for DAEDALUS).
- **Eval:** monthly close-to-close, total return, no slippage/costs (Stage-2 concern; monthly turnover makes costs minor — though the XS short leg adds shorting cost, deepening the L/S result below).
- **Timeframe / hold:** positional / monthly (~21d) — slower than the pilot's B2 swing bucket. If optioned, a 30-60 DTE directional/convex expression, not B3 0DTE.
- **signal_category:** Continuation/Trend family, **-GEX-gated** (vol-amplified/trending book per B1 — opposite the pilot's +GEX reversion). Exact enum deferred, per pilot discipline. (Field is `signal_category`, not the older brief's `signal_class`.)

Flagged for committee (URSA/DAEDALUS): (a) TS hurdle 0 vs T-bill; (b) XS quartile vs tercile; (c) XS long-only vs long/short; (d) stopless canon vs live backstop; (e) skip-1 vs skip-0 (backtested below).

## 3. Anti-Bloat verdict — REJECTED (both arms)

Grounded in `docs/codex-briefs/2026-06-05-master-brief-edge-consolidation.md` (n=11,091) and `docs/architecture/signal-confluence-architecture.md` (lens taxonomy). The kickoff framed this binary (duplicate -> REJECTED vs distinct -> ADDS/ELEVATES). It isn't binary — the two arms required separate calls; the backtest then resolved both to REJECTED.

**Competitive set:** the only LIVE trend incumbent is CTA (`cta-flow-replication`); footprint is a continuation INPUT (not standalone); Holy_Grail-pullback (the "Momentum Continuation" lens) is dead; triple-line-trend-retracement is scrapped. Cash bucket (cap 3) is FULL: CTA-high-score, sell_the_rip, Artemis.

**Pre-backtest call:** TS = REJECTED (duplicate of CTA); XS = ELEVATES-candidate (distinct lens — nothing on the platform ranks a cross-section by relative strength — advance to backtest).

**TS arm -> REJECTED (duplicate of CTA).** The lens taxonomy already collapses "Trend Structure" (CTA) and "Momentum Continuation" (Holy Grail) into adjacent lenses that don't double-count. Monthly TS 12-1 is another absolute-trend lens — "long if own 12-month return > 0" is approximately "price above its rising long-horizon MA," which CTA's 120/200-SMA zones + death-cross filter already encode. TS 12-1 re-fills the dead "Momentum Continuation" slot with a slower restatement of Trend Structure. Backtest quantified the overlap (section 4): TS-long state agrees with price>200-SMA 82-86% of the time. Worse vehicle fit than CTA (binary always-on/off state vs CTA's discrete volume-confirmed entries). Cash cap full; the only sane eviction is CTA, but TS is a worse CTA. No path.

**XS arm -> REJECTED (no Stage-1 edge).** The distinct-lens thesis was real, but the backtest killed the EDGE: the long/short top-vs-bottom-quartile spread (the clean beta-stripped test of relative strength) is approximately 0/negative over 26 years and across all three sub-periods; long-only top quartile does not beat equal-weight-all or SPY (it is just beta). A confluence input carrying zero cross-sectional alpha has nothing to sharpen, so it can't even earn the ELEVATES.

**Caveat (scope the claim):** the XS negative is partly inherent to the universe — cross-sectional momentum is documented as weak among correlated sector/country indices and stronger in broad single-stock universes (deciles over hundreds of names). This is NOT "momentum the factor is dead." It is "12-1 momentum adds no deployable edge to THIS platform on a LIQUID universe, and the TS arm duplicates CTA." A single-stock XS book (hundreds of names, monthly rebalance, a short leg) is a different, out-of-scope build for an options-convexity platform and would be its own spec — the reason a future XS revisit must change the UNIVERSE, not a parameter.

## 4. Stage-1 backtest — canon

**Method:** pure Python, yfinance daily bars (stooq blocked behind a JS wall — not used), auto_adjust=True (total return), monthly rebalance, 12-1 (skip most recent month), close-to-close, no slippage/costs. Universe per section 2; data 1998-2026. Script: `C:\temp\momentum_backtest.py`. Data validated vs the pilot (SPY 744.39, QQQ 737.95 on 2026-06-22). 9 core sectors from 1998-12, XLRE 2015-10, XLC 2018-06; avg 9.6 sectors eligible/month, 318 rebalances.

**TS (long if own 12-1 > 0, else cash):**

| Series | Mos | CAGR | Sharpe | maxDD | vs buy&hold |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| SPY TS 12-1 long/cash | 329 | 9.2% | 0.81 | -19.4% | B&H 8.5% / 0.62 / -50.8% |
| QQQ TS 12-1 long/cash | 315 | 10.0% | 0.67 | -46.7% | B&H 8.2% / 0.47 / -81.1% |

12-1 ≈ 12-0 (SPY 9.2 vs 8.8; QQQ 10.0 vs 10.0). TS beats B&H on Sharpe purely via drawdown/vol control — exactly what CTA's zones deliver.

**Duplication diagnostic (decisive TS finding):** TS-long state vs price>200-SMA — **SPY 86.0% agreement** (TS-long on 79.0%, >200SMA on 75.4%); **QQQ 82.5%** (78.4% / 73.0%). TS 12-1 says the same thing as a plain 200-SMA trend filter 82-86% of the time -> duplicate of the regime CTA already runs.

**XS (sector basket, rank by 12-1, quartile):**

| Series (12-1) | Mos | CAGR | Sharpe | totRet (26y) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Long-only top quartile | 318 | 9.0% | 0.61 | +890% |
| Equal-weight all sectors | 318 | 8.6% | 0.64 | +796% |
| Long/short (top-bottom Q) | 318 | -0.7% | 0.04 | -16.2% |
| SPY buy & hold (ref) | 329 | 8.5% | 0.62 | +841% |

Long-only top-Q does NOT beat equal-weight-all (slightly worse Sharpe, from concentration). The long/short spread ≈ 0/negative. 12-0 L/S also ~0 (0.2% CAGR).

**Sub-period robustness (XS 12-1):**

| Period | XS L/S CAGR | XS Long CAGR |
|---|---|---|
| 1999-2009 (dotcom+GFC) | -2.9% | +1.8% |
| 2010-2019 | -1.3% | +11.7% |
| 2020-2026 (up-tape) | +4.0% | +16.9% |

L/S negative in two of three regimes, mildly positive only in the recent up-tape. Long-only just tracks the market regime (= beta).

**Read:** TS momentum = a slower 200-SMA trend filter (82-86% state overlap with what CTA runs); its benefit is drawdown control CTA's zones already provide. XS sector momentum carries no relative-strength alpha on this liquid basket (L/S ≈ 0 across 26y + sub-periods; long-only = beta). Both arms fail their respective tests. Unlike RSI-2 (real Stage-1 canon edge, parked on B2), momentum is killed at Stage 1 and never reaches the B2 block; modeling options costs on a slow monthly factor (especially the XS short leg) would only deepen the rejection.
